The 5 Gaps

Every restaurant has some combination of these. Your job is to find which ones apply, quantify them, and help the merchant see them clearly. This is the foundation of every diagnostic conversation.

Gap 1

Data Gap

Not capturing enough customer data. Most restaurants only know ~25% of their diners. The other 75% walk out as strangers — no name, no contact, no way to reach them again.

"How many diners per month? How many do you actually have data on?"
In practice: Restaurant serves 5,000 diners/month. 500 reservation bookings. They think they "know" 500 customers. In reality, 500 bookers — the other 4,500 walked out invisible.

Primary: Enrolment (grow data) · Supported by: Reserve + eShop (sales channels that capture data)

Gap 2

Insight Gap

Even with some data, it doesn't paint a full picture. Two sub-problems:

  • Data is siloed, not unified — reservation system, POS, delivery platforms each have fragments. None of it connects. A customer who books Saturday and orders Tuesday looks like two different people.
  • Missing in-depth data — how customers discovered you, their proximity to you, why they returned, what made them lapse. The data that drives real decisions doesn't exist.
"Do you know how your customers discover you? Measured or gut feel?"
In practice: Merchant says "mostly Instagram." You ask: "Measured or gut feel?" Always gut feel. If 60% actually come from Google, they're investing in the wrong channel.

Primary: Enrolment (survey data) + CDP (unified data & segmentation)

Gap 3

Engagement Gap

Not doing anything meaningful to bring customers back. Two sub-problems:

  • No data → no insights → no idea how to engage — can't engage customers you don't know. This is the downstream consequence of Gaps 1 and 2.
  • Have data but didn't engage — the data exists, but the restaurant isn't doing anything with it. No campaigns, no outreach, no reason for the customer to come back. Passive, not active.
"What are you doing today to bring customers back?"
In practice: They post on Instagram. But that reaches followers — not the specific customer who dined last Tuesday. 85% of first-timers don't return within a year. Not bad food — no follow-up.

Primary: Email Marketing · Supported by: Enrolment (data to target with)

Gap 4

Revenue Gap

Leaving money on the table across multiple dimensions. Three sub-problems:

  • Not driving repeat sales — no engagement, no retention mechanism. Return visits are left entirely to chance.
  • No organic online sales channel — missing out on corporate orders, group orders, and direct online revenue. These $100+ orders have nowhere to go (or end up on marketplaces at 30% commission).
  • Not maximizing potential — have tools but no strategy or playbook on how to grow. Tools exist but sit idle.
"Do customers order for families, offices, groups? Are you capturing those?"
In practice: Marketplace orders average $20-30 (impulse). Corporate/group orders average $100-120, planned ahead. Completely different customer — and they're not being reached.

Primary: Enrolment (drive 2nd & 3rd visit) + Email Marketing (engagement for repeat + top-of-mind) + eShop (additional sales channel)

Gap 5

Cost Gap

Overpaying for tools that underdeliver — and getting no strategic support. Three sub-problems:

  • Overpaying for fragmented tools — paying $200-500/mo for reservations, $100-300/mo for online ordering, $50-200/mo for loyalty, separately. None connected. Total spend is high, total value is low.
  • Per-product value gap — each tool might seem affordable alone, but probe the value: cost per booking, capture rate, email open rates. Most tools are just operational — they manage, they don't grow.
  • Vendors are tool providers, not partners — when did your vendor last proactively share how you're doing? Offer insights? Help you take action? Most restaurants don't have a revenue partner — just a collection of software subscriptions.
"Walk me through what you're paying across your tools — and more importantly, what you're getting for it."
In practice: Merchant pays $400/mo for reservations, $150/mo for loyalty, $100/mo for email — $650/mo total. Reservation system: $5 per booking, no data flows anywhere. Loyalty: 200 members from 5,000 diners = 4% capture. Email: monthly blasts, no segmentation. Paying $650/mo for tools that don't talk to each other and don't drive growth.

Primary: All Access Plan ($215/mo, everything connected) + Oddle Team (revenue partner, not just a vendor)

The sequence is flexible. The thoroughness is not. Lead with whatever the merchant cares about. But by the end of the diagnostic, cover all five. Checklist, not script.

Sales Philosophy

Diagnose, Don't Pitch

A doctor doesn't walk in and say "you need surgery." They ask questions, run tests, help the patient understand. Then the patient asks for help. That's what we do.

You're here to help a restaurant owner see — with their own numbers and words — where they're leaking revenue, overpaying, or flying blind. The pitch becomes inevitable because they already see the problem.

Questions > Statements

When you tell a merchant "you have a data gap," they can disagree. When you ask "how many of your 5,000 diners do you actually have contact info for?" and they calculate 500 — they can't disagree with their own math.

Two Things You Must Do Throughout

1. Establish F&B credibility. Every question should carry an embedded insight about the restaurant business. They should think "these people really know their stuff."

2. Give value along the way. Drop tips that help even if they don't sign up. Each tip builds trust. See the Value Tips Library in Chapter 5.

Always Lead with the Big Picture

When you introduce Oddle, start with New → Known → Repeat and how Oddle grows revenue organically. Never lead with a specific product. eShop, Reserve, Enrolment — parts of the ecosystem, not the whole story.

Cost-per-booking reality check

"You're paying $[D] for [B] bookings — that's $[D÷B] per booking. Oddle gives you 750 free reservation bookings per month. Anything above 750 is just $0.30 each."

Value Tips Library

Drop these during calls to build trust and show F&B expertise. Each tip gives value even if they don't sign up — and plants a seed for Oddle.

Grow Google reviews automatically

"Every time a customer completes a reservation, places an eShop order, or checks in via Enrolment — an automated email asks for a Google review. You're not asking staff to remember. Every transaction becomes a review opportunity. Restaurants on Oddle grow reviews 3-5x faster because the ask happens at scale, automatically."

Expand your effective sales hours

"A busy restaurant sells lunch 12–1:30pm and dinner 7–9pm. That's 4.5 hours of peak revenue. But delivery customers also want to eat at 12 and 7 — which means orders go out 30 minutes earlier, food is prepped 45 minutes before that. Delivery shifts your kitchen's productive hours earlier: 11am prep for 12pm lunch, 5:30pm prep for 7pm dinner. That's 1.5 to 2 extra sales hours a day your kitchen is already staffed for — revenue you're currently leaving on the table."

Unique customers engaged — why send weekly

"Open rate on Oddle averages 30%. Send once a month to 1,000 customers — you engage 300. But send weekly? By month-end, even at 30% per send, the unique customers who opened at least one email is around 700. 70% engaged — because the person who skipped email #1 might open #2 or #3.

And the unsubscribe fear? Most unsubscribes happen on the very first email someone receives. After that, the rate barely moves. Sending more doesn't mean losing more — it means reaching more."

Google search ranking

"Google your name. No direct ordering link? You're losing orders to marketplaces who rank for YOUR name."

Reviews > Followers

"Someone choosing dinner tonight is Googling, not scrolling Instagram. 100 reviews at 4.5 stars > 10K followers."

Delivery fee psychology

"$21 delivery fee kills conversion. Mark up food 15% + charge $7 delivery = same economics, way more orders."

Corporate order opportunity

"Corporates Google '[cuisine] catering near me.' No ordering page? That $150 order goes elsewhere."

The 2nd visit is everything

"85% don't return in a year. 2nd visit → 30-40% chance of 3rd. All energy → driving that 2nd visit."

Off-peak hack

"Wednesday quiet? Nearby customers → midweek offer → 1,000 emails → 50 claims → 30 extra covers."

Weekend revenue

"Kitchen preps anyway. Party platters via online ordering. 1-2 orders at $200-500 = $2-4K/month new."

Fixed cost leverage

"Rent and staff are fixed for 50 or 100 customers. Every additional = near-pure margin. Data capture + repeat = highest ROI."

Objection Library

Quick-reference responses organized by common objection. Use during calls.

"Your eShop is more expensive"

Angle 1: "Can your sales be higher? Logistics issues? Is data unified with dine-in?" — almost always no.

Angle 2 — do the math with them: "Say your online sales are $10K/month. First $3K is commission-free. The 6.5% only applies to the $7K above that — $455. Now weigh that against what you get: if we drive even $500 more in online sales through abandoned cart recovery and corporate orders… if Oddle Reserve replaces your current system and saves you that subscription… if Enrolment captures your dine-in data and email campaigns bring customers back for a second visit, growing your dine-in revenue… does the combined value outweigh $455?"

Angle 3: "Compare total stack to Oddle. $215 for everything vs $X disconnected."

"I already have loyalty"

"How many new members/month? Return rate? Using the data?" Usually idle. "Program isn't the problem — is it driving outcomes?"

"No time for another tool"

"Dedicated success manager for 45-60 days. They handle setup, campaigns, optimization. After that, automations."

"Start with one product?"

"Absolutely. Most do. The rest is there when ready. No new contract."

"My vendor has an account manager"

"When did they last proactively share a report? Suggest a campaign? Responding to tickets ≠ driving growth."

"Need to see it at a restaurant like mine"

Social proof ask. Name 2-3 similar restaurants, share high-level results, offer to show public pages.

"$215 is a lot right now"

"That's ~$7/day. Rent and manpower are fixed. Every extra customer is near-pure margin. 2-3 extra orders/month — conservative — pays for itself. And the database compounds."
